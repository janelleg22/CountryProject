Cuisine

Italy is a country that tantalises the senses, a land where the culinary experience is as rich and layered as its history. Italian cuisine, a vibrant tapestry of tastes and traditions, is a journey of discovery, with each region offering its unique flavours and culinary techniques. The heart of Italian food lies in its simplicity, using high-quality, fresh ingredients to create dishes that have stood the test of time.

Margherita Pizza

In 18th-century Naples, pizza existed as a simple flatbread base, popular among the poor and sold cheaply by street vendors. In 1889, Queen Margherita of Savoy tried pizza and enjoyed it, prompting Chef Raffaele Esposito to create a special version for her. He topped the base with mozzarella, tomatoes, and basil, symbolizing the Italian flag's red, white, and green colors. This creation became both the queen's favorite pizza and the origin of the now-famous Margherita pizza.

Palio Di Siena

The Palio di Siena is a historic horse race held twice annually in Siena, Italy, on July 2 and August 16, honoring the Virgin Mary. Ten of Siena's 17 contrade (districts) compete in a thrilling 90-second race around the Piazza del Campo, preceded by a grand medieval parade and horse blessings.Deep rivalries and centuries-old traditions define the event, with the winning contrada earning the coveted Palio banner and celebrating with jubilant festivities. The Palio is a vibrant expression of Siena's heritage and community pride.
